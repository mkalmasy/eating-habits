Margaret Haney

I know of a few resources, like I know that you can get specialized food plans. It would be nice if they made the information more accessible or if they made the calorie counts or the breakdown of what’s in the food like grams of sugar available for certain foods just because you’re not always sure what you’re eating exactly.

Ashley Castrejon

I think they try to provide information to students about healthy options, like with the symbols that they place on things to tell you if it’s a vegetarian or gluten-free option, but I don’t think there’s anything targeted specifically towards healthy eating other than little “healthy tips” signs on napkin holders that I see.

Joey Bardon

If I’m going to eat on campus, I go to Talley because they have more vegetarian options there. The dining halls don’t really have a lot of options, just salad or pizza, so I mostly just eat at One Earth and Los Lobos.

Margaret Haney

I eat at Fountain and Talley a lot. I especially go to Talley just because it’s so close to where I live, so it’s really convenient, and I also like places like Jason’s Deli and One Earth just because they have healthier food. I go to Fountain because the menu is always changing so there’s more of a variety.

NC State Dining provides Nutrition Facts for every meal provided at the dining halls, as well as ingredients for any additional student allergens or dietary preferences. Additionally, the menu is connected to My Fitness Pal so students can accurately log everything they eat.
